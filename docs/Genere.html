Fictional


The Great Gatsby

A tragic tale of love, wealth, and the American Dream set in the Roaring Twenties. Jay Gatsby’s obsession with rekindling his romance with Daisy Buchanan highlights the emptiness of material success. Fitzgerald’s lyrical writing captures both the glamour and moral decay of high society.

★ ★ ★ ★ ★

To Kill A Mockingbird

A powerful novel about racism and justice in the Deep South, told through the eyes of Scout Finch. Her father, Atticus, defends a Black man wrongly accused of a crime, revealing deep-seated prejudice. The story beautifully blends childhood innocence with serious social themes.

★ ★ ★ ★ ★

Brave New World

A dystopian vision of a society where people are engineered for happiness at the cost of individuality. In this world of constant pleasure and control, true emotions and free will are lost. Huxley’s novel warns about the dangers of technology, consumerism, and authoritarianism.

★ ★ ★ ★ ★


Non-Fictional


Sapiens

A fascinating journey through human history, from early hunter-gatherers to modern civilization. Harari explores how revolutions in cognition, agriculture, and science shaped our societies. The book challenges conventional views on progress, culture, and the future of humanity.

★ ★ ★ ★ ★

Into The Wild

The true story of Christopher McCandless, who abandoned society to live in the Alaskan wilderness. Krakauer explores his motivations, idealism, and the risks of isolation. It’s both an adventure story and a deep reflection on freedom, survival, and the human spirit.

★ ★ ★ ★ ★

The Diary Of A Young Girl

Anne Frank’s deeply moving diary documents her life in hiding during WWII. She shares her thoughts, fears, and hopes while facing the horrors of war. Her words remain a testament to resilience, innocence, and the cruelty of discrimination.

★ ★ ★ ★ ★


Crime


The Girl With The Dragon Tattoo

**The Girl with the Dragon Tattoo** is a gripping mystery thriller by Stieg Larsson. It follows journalist Mikael Blomkvist and hacker Lisbeth Salander as they uncover dark family secrets while investigating a decades-old disappearance. The novel blends crime, corruption, and psychological depth, making it a compelling and intense read.

★ ★ ★ ★ ★

Big Little Lies

**Big Little Lies** by Liane Moriarty is a gripping psychological thriller that unravels secrets, lies, and the complexities of female friendships. Set in an upscale seaside town, the story follows three women whose seemingly perfect lives hide dark truths. With sharp humor and deep emotional layers, it builds up to a shocking and suspenseful climax.

★ ★ ★ ★ ★

The Silence of the Lambs

**The Silence of the Lambs** by Thomas Harris is a chilling psychological thriller that follows FBI trainee Clarice Starling as she seeks help from the brilliant yet terrifying cannibal, Dr. Hannibal Lecter, to catch a serial killer. The novel masterfully blends suspense, crime, and psychological manipulation. With its intense atmosphere and unforgettable characters, it remains a classic in the thriller genre.

★ ★ ★ ★ ★


Horror


Dracula

**Dracula** by Bram Stoker is a gothic horror classic that introduced the iconic vampire Count Dracula. Told through journal entries and letters, it follows Jonathan Harker and his allies as they battle the ancient and sinister vampire. The novel blends suspense, mystery, and supernatural terror, shaping modern vampire lore. Its eerie atmosphere and timeless themes make it a must-read for horror fans.

★ ★ ★ ★ ★

The Exorcist

**The Exorcist** by William Peter Blatty is a terrifying horror novel about a young girl, Regan, who becomes possessed by a demonic force. As her condition worsens, her mother seeks help from priests to perform an exorcism. Blending psychological suspense with supernatural horror, the novel explores faith, evil, and fear. Its chilling atmosphere and shocking scenes make it one of the most haunting books ever written.

★ ★ ★ ★ ★

The Amityville Horror

**The Amityville Horror** by Jay Anson is a chilling account of a family’s real-life paranormal experiences in their new home. Shortly after moving in, the Lutz family is tormented by strange noises, eerie visions, and violent supernatural forces. The novel blends horror and suspense, leaving readers questioning the truth behind the events. Whether fact or fiction, it remains one of the most famous haunted house stories ever told.

★ ★ ★ ★ ★


Romance


Me Before You

**Me Before You** by Jojo Moyes is an emotional romance about Louisa, a quirky young woman, who becomes a caregiver for Will, a wealthy man paralyzed from an accident. As their bond deepens, Louisa tries to show Will that life is still worth living. The novel explores love, loss, and difficult choices, leaving readers heartbroken yet deeply moved.

★ ★ ★ ★ ★

The Notebook

The Notebook by Nicholas Sparks is a heartfelt romance about Noah and Allie, whose love withstands time, separation, and hardships. Told through past and present timelines, it explores themes of true love, fate, and devotion. The novel’s emotional depth and timeless romance make it a beloved classic.

★ ★ ★ ★ ★

Twilight

Twilight by Stephenie Meyer is a paranormal romance that follows Bella Swan, a teenager who falls in love with Edward Cullen, a mysterious vampire. Their love is tested by supernatural dangers and their differences. With its mix of romance, suspense, and fantasy, it became a cultural phenomenon.

★ ★ ★ ★ ★


Fantasy


The Hobbit

**The Hobbit** by J.R.R. Tolkien is a classic fantasy adventure following Bilbo Baggins, a reluctant hobbit who embarks on a quest with dwarves to reclaim their homeland from the dragon Smaug. Along the journey, Bilbo encounters trolls, goblins, and the One Ring, which shapes the fate of Middle-earth. With rich storytelling and unforgettable characters, it’s a timeless tale of bravery and self-discovery.

★ ★ ★ ★ ★

The Wheel of Time

A young hero is destined to confort dark forces**The Wheel of Time** by Robert Jordan is an epic fantasy series set in a richly detailed world where time is cyclical, and destiny weaves heroes into a grand battle between good and evil. It follows Rand al’Thor and his companions as they discover their fates in the struggle against the Dark One. Blending mythology, intricate world-building, and deep character arcs, the series is a cornerstone of modern fantasy.

★ ★ ★ ★ ★

Eragon

**Eragon** by Christopher Paolini is a thrilling fantasy novel about a farm boy who discovers a mysterious dragon egg, setting him on a path to become a Dragon Rider. As he trains in magic and combat, he joins a rebellion against the evil King Galbatorix. Filled with adventure, mythical creatures, and epic battles, the novel is a captivating tale of destiny and heroism.

★ ★ ★ ★ ★
